Fresno county produces the greatest amount of agricultural product, measured in sales, of any county in California. There are 1.88 million acres of productive farmland in the county, representing almost half of the county's 3.84 million acres. There are more than 350 different crops, and the direct contribution to the California economy is over $5.6 billion.

The top crops are grapes ($1.1 billion), almonds ($860 million), tomatoes ($500 million), peaches ($438 million), garlic ($294 million) and alfalfa ($129 million).
